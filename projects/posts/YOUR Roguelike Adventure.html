Your Roguelike Adventure

[ Download on itch.io ]

Developing "Your Roguelike Adventure" was part of my club activities at UCI. It helped me grow to understand what working on a team is truly like: learning to communicate as effectively as possible, making safe choices early in programming, and being able to make evaluative compromises with the rest of my team. I programmed much of the gameplay as well as the fundamental systems inherent to creating content for the gameplay in the future.

This project was my first "official" collaborative effort, aside from fun projects I helped my friends with, and I worked as the lead programmer in a team of six. It was a learning experience to have to plan ahead in order to make sure the important parts of the code could be a foundation for my fellow developers to pick up on, as well as to make sure to be extra clear in the way that I write my code, and be diligent in communicating anything that wasn't quite there. I also got the opportunity to experience a good use case for abstract classes, as well as to really hunker down and focus on making player movement and control simple yet satisfying, as well as visually responsive.
